# ## AI Strategy Survey: 4-Level Decision Tree

This tool guides a university leader from a general question to a specific, actionable starting point.

### **Level 1: Primary Goal**

**Question 1:** "What is your primary goal today?"

- **[A] Find Opportunities:** "How can AI create new value, increase revenue, or improve efficiency?"
    
    - `Go to Path A, Level 2`
        
- **[B] Manage Risk:** "How do we use AI without exposing the university to data breaches, lawsuits, or reputational damage?"
    
    - `Go to Path B, Level 2`
        
- **[C] Justify Investment:** "How do we measure ROI, build a business case, and see what our competitors are doing?"
    
    - `Go to Path C, Level 2`
        

### **[A] Path A: The Explorer (Find Opportunities)**

**Level 2: Scoping vs. Ideation**

- **Insight:** "A good start is critical. Research shows **80% of AI projects fail to deliver value**, frequently because of poor initial scoping."
    
- **Question 2:** "Do you have a specific problem to solve, or are you looking for ideas?"
    
    - **[A1] "I'm looking for ideas."**
        
        - `Go to Path A1, Level 3`
            
    - **[A2] "I have a specific problem to solve."**
        
        - `Go to Path A2, Level 3`
            

**Level 3 (Path A1): Learning from Peers**

- **Insight:** "The best ideas come from proven models. One university saw a **3.3% enrollment increase** after launching an AI-powered student support chatbot."
    
- **Question 3:** "To help you ideate, do you want to explore proven _revenue-generating_ use cases or learn from _costly public failures_?"
    
    - **[A1.1] Learn from Successes:**
        
        - `Go to Path A1.1, Level 4`
            
    - **[A1.2] Learn from Failures:**
        
        - `Go to Path A1.2, Level 4`
            

**Level 3 (Path A2): Formalizing a First Project**

- **Insight:** "This is the 'first project' hurdle. The goal is to pick a high-value, low-complexity 'quick win' that aligns with a core university mission."
    
- **Question 3:** "Is your problem primarily _student-facing_ (e.g., support, retention) or _staff-facing_ (e.g., automating admin, HR, finance)?"
    
    - **[A2.1] Student-Facing:**
        
        - `Go to Path A2.1, Level 4`
            
    - **[A2.2] Staff-Facing:**
        
        - `Go to Path A2.2, Level 4`
            

**Level 4 (Path A1.1): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "You need to see what 'good' looks like. The GSU case proves that focusing on 'soft' metrics (student retention) can deliver the strongest 'hard' financial returns (enrollment)."
    
- **Output:** **`Peer_Analysis_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (Path A1.2): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "Smart. The Deakin chatbot failure wasn't an AI problem; it was a _data_ problem. They connected it to an unvetted knowledge base, causing massive reputational damage."
    
- **Output:** **`Peer_Analysis_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (Path A2.1): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "This is a high-value path. This charter is the first step to formalize your scope, value, and complexity for leadership review."
    
- **Output:** **`MVAIP_Charter_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (Path A2.2): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "This is a great, low-risk way to build capability. This charter is the first step to formalize your scope, value, and complexity for leadership review."
    
- **Output:** **`MVAIP_Charter_Template.md`**
    

### **[B] Path B: The Guardian (Manage Risk)**

**Level 2: Governance vs. Security**

- **Insight:** "This is a core strategic function. A good policy _enables_ fast, safe innovation; it doesn't just block it. Our model balances central oversight with faculty-level agility."
    
- **Question 2:** "Is your main concern with _Ethical Risk_ (e.g., bias, fairness, bad decisions) or _Technical Risk_ (e.g., data leaks, privacy breaches)?"
    
    - **[B1] Ethical Risk:**
        
        - `Go to Path B1, Level 3`
            
    - **[B2] Technical Risk:**
        
        - `Go to Path B2, Level 3`
            

**Level 3 (Path B1): Assessing Ethical Risk**

- **Insight:** "Our ethical framework is built on 5 principles, including **'Contestable.'** This is a non-negotiable. It means every student or staff member must have a clear, advertised path to _appeal_ an AI's decision."
    
- **Question 3:** "Are you assessing a _specific project's_ ethical risk, or developing a _department-level policy_?"
    
    - **[B1.1] Assessing a New Project:**
        
        - `Go to Path B1.1, Level 4`
            
    - **[B1.2] Developing Department Policy:**
        
        - `Go to Path B1.2, Level 4`
            

**Level 3 (Path B2): Assessing Data Security**

- **Insight:** "This is our #1 security rule. Our policy **prohibits all staff** from entering **sensitive student or financial (Tier 3/4) data** into _any_ public AI tool, period."
    
- **Question 3:** "Are you planning to use a _public_ AI tool (like ChatGPT) or a _private_, internally-hosted AI model?"
    
    - **[B2.1] Public GenAI Tool:**
        
        - `Go to Path B2.1, Level 4`
            
    - **[B2.2] Private/Internal AI:**
        
        - `Go to Path B2.2, Level 4`
            

**Level 4 (Path B1.1): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "Every AI project, no matter how small, must be assessed. This form is the mandatory first step. It determines if the project is 'Low/Medium' (handled by your team) or 'High' (escalated to central leadership)."
    
- **Output:** **`AI_Risk_Assessment_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (Path B1.2): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "Your department policy should adopt this risk assessment as its core operational tool. This ensures your team can move quickly on low-risk projects while correctly escalating high-risk projects."
    
- **Output:** **`AI_Risk_Assessment_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (B2.1): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "This is a high-risk activity. It's only permissible for _non-sensitive_ data. You must complete this checklist to confirm data classification and mitigate major security risks (e.g., 'Prompt Injection')."
    
- **Output:** **`GenAI_Security_Checklist.md`**
    

**Level 4 (B2.2): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "This is the correct, secure path for sensitive data. You must complete this checklist to ensure the private environment is secure and mitigates risks like 'Data Poisoning'."
    
- **Output:** **`GenAI_Security_Checklist.md`**
    

### **[C] Path C: The Strategist (Justify Investment)**

**Level 2: Project ROI vs. Institutional Strategy**

- **Insight:** "Value and competition are linked. Our peer analysis shows we are at an 'Operational' level (using AI in silos), while key competitors are 'Systemic' (integrated strategy). This is mainly due to a **'Technology Deficit'** in high-power computing."
    
- **Question 2:** "Is your focus on measuring the value of a _specific project_ (ROI) or assessing the _university's overall competitive strategy_?"
    
    - **[C1] Project Value (ROI):**
        
        - `Go to Path C1, Level 3`
            
    - **[C2] Overall Strategy (Peers):**
        
        - `Go to Path C2, Level 3`
            

**Level 3 (Path C1): Measuring Hybrid ROI**

- **Insight:** "Traditional ROI models fail for AI. The GSU case proved that focusing on **'Soft ROI'** (like student retention) is what ultimately delivered the **'Hard ROI'** (a 3.3% enrollment increase)."
    
- **Question 3:** "Are you _building_ a new business case (pre-project), or _evaluating_ an existing project (post-project)?"
    
    - **[C1.1] Building a New Business Case:**
        
        - `Go to Path C1.1, Level 4`
            
    - **[C1.2] Evaluating an Existing Project:**
        
        - `Go to Path C1.2, Level 4`
            

**Level 3 (Path C2): Addressing the Competitive Gap**

- **Insight:** "This is the core strategic question. Our 'Technology Deficit' means we _cannot_ currently compete with peer infrastructure (like the multi-million dollar AI clusters at Monash and Sydney) on large-scale model research."
    
- **Question 3:** "This gap forces a fundamental 5-year capital investment choice. Is this a conversation you are preparing for at the senior leadership level?"
    
    - **[C2.1] Yes, this is for senior leadership.**
        
        - `Go to Path C2.1, Level 4`
            
    - **[C2.2] No, I am just gathering information.**
        
        - `Go to Path C2.2, Level 4`
            

**Level 4 (C1.1): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "Your business case must use this Hybrid ROI model. You must define mission-critical 'Soft ROI' metrics (e.g., faculty time reclaimed, student satisfaction) from Day 1 to prove true value."
    
- **Output:** **`Value_Realization_Plan_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (C1.2): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "This framework will help you measure the _true_ value of the project. Focus on the mission-critical 'Soft ROI' metrics (e.g., student satisfaction), not just basic cost savings."
    
- **Output:** **`Value_Realization_Plan_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (C2.1): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "This is the most critical strategic conversation. This brief outlines the three capital investment choices leadership must make: 1) **Sovereign Infrastructure** (compete), 2) **Enterprise Partnership** (depend), or 3) **Niche Leadership** (govern)."
    
- **Output:** **`Strategic_Choice_Brief_Template.md`**
    

**Level 4 (C2.2): Output**

- **Diagnosis:** "Understood. This brief outlines the critical decision leadership must make, driven by the competitive gap. This is the central strategic document to review."
    
- **Output:** **`Strategic_Choice_Brief_Template.md`**
