# DRAFT - AI Readiness Self-Diagnostic

#Org
#CDN


Purpose: Help leaders quickly assess their organisation’s AI maturity across five core dimensions — and guide them to the right next action.

 

STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

Section	Focus Area	No. of Questions	Max Score

1	Strategy & Leadership	2	10

2	Data & Infrastructure	2	10

3	Governance & Risk	2	10

4	People & Capability	2	10

5	Value Realisation	2	10

TOTAL		10 Questions	50 Points

Each question uses a 1–5 scale, where:

-	1 = Not in place / not started

-	2 = Early awareness / informal effort

-	3 = Partially implemented / developing

-	4 = Mostly implemented / operating well

-	5 = Fully embedded / continuous improvement

 

 

SECTION 1 – Strategy & Leadership

Q1. How clearly defined is your organisation’s AI strategy and roadmap?

1️⃣ No formal AI strategy or vision.

2️⃣ Initial discussions underway, but no defined roadmap.

3️⃣ Strategy drafted or being refined, some initiatives aligned.

4️⃣ Clear strategy endorsed by leadership, aligned to business priorities.

5️⃣ Enterprise-wide AI strategy fully embedded and regularly reviewed.

Q2. How aligned and engaged is executive leadership around AI’s role in business transformation?

1️⃣ Limited leadership awareness or buy-in.

2️⃣ Some curiosity, but fragmented understanding.

3️⃣ Key leaders support AI in principle, but not driving it.

4️⃣ Strong executive sponsorship with clear accountability.

5️⃣ Leadership fully engaged and modelling AI-driven decision-making.

 

 

SECTION 2 – Data & Infrastructure

Q3. How would you describe the quality and accessibility of data in your organisation?

1️⃣ Data is siloed, inconsistent, and difficult to access.

2️⃣ Some progress in integration, but gaps remain.

3️⃣ Core systems connected, but quality varies.

4️⃣ Reliable, well-managed data accessible across most business units.

5️⃣ Enterprise-wide, trusted data ecosystem designed for AI enablement.

Q4. How well is your technology infrastructure prepared to support AI solutions (e.g., integration, cloud, compute)?

1️⃣ Legacy systems not suited for AI workloads.

2️⃣ Basic cloud or analytics tools in place, limited AI support.

3️⃣ Modern infrastructure partially ready for AI.

4️⃣ Strong foundation — scalable and secure.

5️⃣ Fully optimised and integrated AI-ready environment.

 



 

SECTION 3 – Governance & Risk

Q5. How formalised is your approach to AI governance, ethics, and risk management?

1️⃣ None – no policies or frameworks.

2️⃣ Early awareness, discussions starting.

3️⃣ Draft or partial frameworks exist, but limited enforcement.

4️⃣ Governance in place with clear roles and oversight.

5️⃣ Mature governance embedded, reviewed regularly, and transparent.

Q6. How confident are you in your organisation’s ability to manage AI compliance and ethical considerations (privacy, bias, transparency)?

1️⃣ No established practices.

2️⃣ Ad-hoc compliance efforts, reactive.

3️⃣ Basic frameworks in development.

4️⃣ Proactive management and audit processes in place.

5️⃣ Continuous ethical oversight integrated with operations.

 

 

SECTION 4 – People & Capability

Q7. How equipped is your workforce to engage with and leverage AI responsibly?

1️⃣ No formal training or awareness.

2️⃣ Some isolated training or experimentation.

3️⃣ Targeted skill development underway.

4️⃣ AI literacy embedded across key roles.

5️⃣ Continuous learning culture with widespread capability.

Q8. How well is change management supporting AI adoption (communication, engagement, role clarity)?

1️⃣ Change readiness not considered.

2️⃣ Ad-hoc communication, limited planning.

3️⃣ Structured change planning in some areas.

4️⃣ Managed program with clear messaging and engagement.

5️⃣ Proactive culture and behavioural alignment driving adoption.

 

 

SECTION 5 – Value Realisation

Q9. How effectively are you identifying, prioritising, and tracking AI use cases?

1️⃣ No defined process or portfolio management.

2️⃣ Ad-hoc experimentation, unclear ROI.

3️⃣ Some prioritisation and benefit tracking emerging.

4️⃣ Structured pipeline linked to business value.

5️⃣ Mature portfolio management and outcome measurement.

Q10. How confident are you that your AI initiatives are delivering measurable business or operational value?

1️⃣ No measurement or outcomes tracked.

2️⃣ Limited anecdotal evidence only.

3️⃣ Early evidence of ROI in select areas.

4️⃣ Clear metrics, tracked across multiple functions.

5️⃣ Regularly reported enterprise-wide AI value realisation.

 

 

SCORING LOGIC

Step 1: Assign numeric values (1–5) to each answer.

Step 2: Sum the 10 question scores (max = 50).

Step 3: Categorise total into one of four readiness levels.

Score Range	Level	Descriptor	Recommended CTA

10–20	🟠 Emerging	Exploring AI concepts; need clarity on vision, governance, and data foundations.	Invite to “AI Starter Workshop” — where to begin, establish roadmap.

21–30	🟡 Developing	Early initiatives and pilots underway; focus needed on governance and business alignment.	Offer “AI Readiness Accelerator” (2-week advisory engagement).

31–40	🟢 Established	Solid foundation in place, ready to scale and measure value.	Suggest “AI Value Realisation & Capability Uplift” engagement.

41–50	🔵 Advanced	Enterprise-level integration with governance and measurable ROI.	Invite to partner on thought leadership or co-create case studies.
