Best Practices and Policy Rules for Using Public Generative AI

The overarching policy rule across various institutions and regulatory frameworks is the **strict prohibition** on entering sensitive data into public, third-party generative AI services. This is due to the inherent risk of data leakage, loss of control, and subsequent security and privacy violations.

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|Policy Rule / Best Practice|Description and Rationale|Source(s)|
|**Mandatory Human Review**|All Generative AI (GenAI) outputs used for official purposes must undergo **human review and verification** before finalization or distribution, regardless of the confidence the output inspires. Users must **always fact-check** AI-generated content against reliable sources.||
|**Transparency and Disclosure**|**Clear notification** must be provided when AI systems are being used or when GenAI has contributed to public-facing or official internal content. Disclosure can be as simple as noting **"AI-assisted"** or **"Draft generated with AI assistance and reviewed by [name]"**.||
|**Content and Role Verification**|Employees must be trained to **verify information** and **avoid over-reliance** on AI. Always treat the AI as a drafting tool, not a replacement for professional judgment and accountability, as the employee remains **fully responsible** for all content used in an official capacity.||
|**Tool Approval and Use Case Vetting**|Employees must **confirm the AI tool is on their organization’s approved list** for the specific purpose intended. Use should align with job responsibilities and the agency/department mission. Unauthorized use of AI is classified as **"shadow AI"**, which creates security blind spots and risks data leakage.||
|**Ethical Awareness and Reporting**|Employees must enhance their **awareness of personal information protection** and be on alert for potential safety risks. They should be trained to **recognize and report** biased, inappropriate, or concerning outputs to the designated official.||
|**Prompt Best Practices**|Write clear, **professional prompts** that specify the parameters of the request. Do not include sensitive, confidential, or private information in prompts.||

Prohibited Data Types in Public Generative AI

The input of any data classified as **Tier 2 (Internal/Sensitive), Tier 3 (Restricted/Confidential), or Tier 4 (Highly Restricted/Critical)** into any publicly available, third-party generative AI service (such as ChatGPT, Google Gemini, or Claude) is **strictly prohibited**. This rule is enforced because the underlying mechanism of these tools captures the data and may use it for training future products, resulting in transmission outside of organizational or sovereign control.

Specifically prohibited categories of data include, but are not limited to:

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|Prohibited Data Type|Examples and Context|Source(s)|
|**Personally Identifiable Information (PII) / Private Information**|Names, email addresses, student ID numbers, phone numbers, images, audio recordings, login credentials, and other sensitive personal data. Uploading citizen correspondence with personal details for analysis is inappropriate.||
|**Highly Restricted/Critical Data**|Full Social Security Numbers (SSNs), government-issued identifiers (passport/driver's license), **Protected Health Information (PHI)** under HIPAA, **credit card and bank account numbers** (PCI data), biometric data, and private encryption keys.||
|**Confidential or Commercial-in-Confidence Data**|Company or University **Intellectual Property (IP)**, proprietary source code, internal financial projections, trade secrets, confidential internal documents, and strategic plans.||
|**Sensitive Research Data**|**Human data**, confidential research data, or information that is **Indigenous Cultural and Intellectual Property**. Researchers must not use GenAI to assess **peer review material** (e.g., grants, manuscripts, ethics applications), as this breaches confidentiality.||
|**Security Sensitive Information**|Information about **security systems, vulnerabilities, or access controls**. This also includes **login credentials** and system access details.||

Employees are advised that if they would not be comfortable sharing information publicly on the Internet, it is not appropriate for third-party AI tools.
