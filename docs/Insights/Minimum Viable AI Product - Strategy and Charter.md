The **Minimum Viable AI Product (MVAIP)** is a crucial strategic concept within the AI governance and adoption roadmap, specifically designed to initiate the "Crawl" or Foundation phase of institutional transformation.

Definition of a Minimum Viable AI Product (MVAIP)

Based on the sources, an MVAIP is defined by its scope and purpose, prioritizing foundational learning and demonstrable value:

1. **A Small-Scale, Hypothesis-Driven Project:** The MVAIP is a **small-scale, tightly scoped, hypothesis-driven project** designed for rapid learning and value demonstration. It serves as a **scientific instrument** used to test a critical hypothesis and to "**collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort**". Its primary goal is **learning, not just delivery**.

2. **Strategic Alignment ("Quick Win"):** MVAIPs are strategically selected from the **"Quick Wins" quadrant** of the Value vs. Complexity Prioritization Matrix. These projects offer substantial benefits with minimal investment and risk. Examples include automating administrative tasks for faculty (like generating course materials) or staff.

3. **Building Capacity and Buy-In:** MVAIPs are intended to solve immediate, deeply felt pain points, thereby generating immediate goodwill and **building institutional capability and confidence**. Successful initial projects free up human capacity needed for later, more complex "Strategic Initiatives".

The MVAIP charter is the primary initiation document used to operationalize the early phases of the AI roadmap and is subject to review by the AI Steering Committee.

Key Questions a Project Lead Must Answer Before Starting

Before an MVAIP project commences, the project lead is required to address several critical strategic, data, and ethical questions, typically documented in the **MVAIP Charter**, the **Value Realization Plan**, and the **AI Risk Assessment Form**.
