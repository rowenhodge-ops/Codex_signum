1. Ethical/Governance Failure in Assessment (Academic Integrity Crisis)

The most pervasive governance failure is the inability of Australian universities to maintain academic integrity standards against the wholesale, unchecked use of generative AI (GenAI) in student assessment, which poses an existential threat to the value of the degree.

|Specific Failure/Risk|Context and Implication|Source(s)|

2. Ethical Risk of Bias in Admissions and Predictive Systems

Algorithms, particularly those used for high-stakes decision-making (like admissions or grading), face the significant ethical risk of **perpetuating or amplifying existing societal biases**.

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|Specific Failure/Risk|Context and Implication for Bias|Source(s)|
|**Bias in Algorithmic Scoring Systems**|In the UK (England and Ireland), algorithmic scoring systems were implemented to standardize results and prevent grade inflation. However, these systems were **criticized for disproportionately downgrading students from certain groups** (e.g., those attending disadvantaged schools) by overemphasizing past institutional performance. This failure caused **psychological distress**, perceptions of unfairness, and led to **legal challenges and widespread protests**.|,,,.|
|**Disadvantaging Underrepresented Groups in Assessment**|If an AI system for grading essays is trained predominantly on samples from native English speakers, it may **unfairly penalize non-native speakers** due to differences in syntax or idiomatic expressions. Similarly, AI plagiarism detectors have been found to **disproportionately flag the writing of non-native English speakers** as AI-generated, creating potential for false accusations of misconduct against vulnerable students. Research also shows that non-native English speakers **use and adopt GenAI more readily** than native speakers, potentially exacerbating the risk of academic misconduct flags.|,,,.|

The application of AI in tracking student behaviour, while often intended to improve engagement, raises significant ethical and privacy concerns related to mass surveillance and autonomy.

|---|---|---|
