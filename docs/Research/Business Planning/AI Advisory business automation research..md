This is the correct mindset. The "billionaire unicorn" philosophy, when applied to a solo consultancy, is not about valuation; it's about building a system of leverage. The goal is to achieve a 10x-100x ratio of output (client value, revenue) to input (your personal time).

Your IP (the "Consultancy OS") and your business operations must be built from Day 1 on this principle. The research should not be "what tools to use," but "how to build asynchronous, automated systems."

Here are the research topics, structured around this philosophy.

### 1. Research: Automating Delivery (The "Consultancy OS" Playbook)

This is about building your "product." Your goal is to never deliver the same service twice. The first (Beta) delivery is done by you; the second is done by your Playbook (executed by your "Delivery Lead").

- Service Productization Architecture
    

- Deep Dive: Research the "Service SKU" model. How to deconstruct your $45k Roadmap and $150k Pilot into a fixed-price, fixed-scope "product" with a bill of materials (e.g., 3 workshops, 1 synthesis report, 1 SOW) and a documented "assembly line."
    
- Sub-topics: The E-Myth (Gerber) and Built to Sell (Warrillow) as operational frameworks. How to design a service that a certified partner can deliver 80% of, with you only providing the final 20% (the "genius" work).
    

- Zero-Shot Process Capture (The "Beta Pilot")
    

- Deep Dive: How to automatically create the V1 Playbook while delivering the Beta Pilot, without the friction of manual documentation.
    
- Sub-topics: Research "process capture" tools (e.g., Scribe, Tango, Guidde) that watch your screen and auto-generate "how-to" guides for your partners. Research using AI transcription (e.g., Fireflies, descript) on all client workshops to create an instant, searchable knowledge base.
    

- "Just-in-Time" Partner Enablement (The "Delivery Lead" Bot)
    

- Deep Dive: How to onboard your "Delivery Lead" and "Backup Strategist" to your methodology asynchronously. You cannot afford to spend 20 synchronous hours in training.
    
- Sub-topics: Research building a "Playbook Bot"—an internal RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) agent. Feed all your Beta project docs, process guides, and SOWs into a vector store and build a simple GPT/chatbot front-end. Your partners' first question is always to the bot, not to you.
    

- Generative AI in Service Delivery (The "OS" Stack)
    

- Deep Dive: How to build your specific agentic workflows to execute the $45k Roadmap.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Agentic Research & Synthesis: Comparative analysis of multi-agent frameworks (e.g., CrewAI, LangGraph) for the specific task of your "Market Analysis Agent"—taking a client vertical, scraping competitors/reports, and drafting a synthesized "Key Themes" document.
    
- Deliverable Generation: Research structured output from LLMs. How to create a workflow that takes your raw bullet-point insights and auto-populates them into your proprietary, branded KPMG-quality PowerPoint and Word templates?
    
- Generative Process Mining: Research how to find the pilot opportunity faster. How to use LLMs to analyze client-provided artifacts (process logs, interview transcripts, system reports) to suggest the top 3 high-ROI automation opportunities, rather than you finding them manually.
    

### 2. Research: Automating the Business (The "Practice OS")

This is about making the business run itself. Your goal is to have zero administrative overhead.

- "No-Code" Integration (The Central Nervous System)
    

- Deep Dive: How to create a fully automated "Client-to-Project" pipeline.
    
- Sub-topics: Research the integration stack (e.g., Zapier, Make, n8n). The ideal flow: Client signs SOW (e.g., DocuSign) -> Triggers Stripe payment -> Triggers Google Drive/SharePoint folder creation (with all Playbook templates) -> Triggers Asana/Trello project setup (with all standard tasks) -> Triggers Slack/Teams channel creation and partner invites.
    

- AI-Agent-Based Administration
    

- Deep Dive: How to automate all non-billable financial and project management tasks.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Financial Admin: Research AI-native bookkeeping (e.g., Hnry, or AI layers on Xero) to auto-chase invoices (with the 2% late fee), categorize expenses, and manage/pay your partner retainers.
    
- Project Governance Agent: Research how to build an agent that scrapes Asana, Slack, and your "Delivery Lead's" check-ins to generate the first draft of the weekly client status report for your review.
    

### 3. Research: Automating Growth (The "Growth OS")

This is about 1-to-many, asynchronous selling. Your goal is to have clients 80% "sold" before they ever speak to you.

- "Thought Leadership" Content Engine
    

- Deep Dive: How to turn your one Beta Pilot project into six months of high-leverage marketing content.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Content Atomization: Research the "hub-and-spoke" content model. One (1) 2,000-word "Beta Case Study" becomes: ten (10) LinkedIn posts, three (3) blog articles (on different angles), one (1) guest post for the HEI journal, and one (1) webinar script.
    
- Generative Repurposing: Research AI tools to execute the atomization (e.g., using AI summarizers for text, Opus Clip/Klap for turning a single webinar into 20 social videos).
    

- Asynchronous Sales Funnel
    

- Deep Dive: How to build your "sanitized demo" funnel to qualify leads while you sleep.
    
- Sub-topics: Research the tech stack (e.g., Webflow/Framer for landing page -> ConvertKit/Mailchimp for email capture -> automated email sequence that delivers the demo). The demo itself is the salesperson.
    

- AI-Powered Lead Qualification
    

- Deep Dive: How to build a "front-door" AI agent that handles the first 3-5 email interactions with an inbound lead.
    
- Sub-topics: The agent's only job is to qualify (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) and, if they pass, provide your Calendly link. This ensures you only take synchronous calls with pre-sold, high-value prospects.
    

- "Client-as-Marketer" Flywheel
    

- Deep Dive: How to automate the gathering and deployment of social proof.
    
- Sub-topics: Research testimonial-gathering platforms (e.g., Senja, Testimonial.to) that can be automatically triggered 30 days after a successful pilot (linked from your project management tool) to capture and syndicate reviews.
