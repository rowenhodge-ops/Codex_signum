## 1. Executive Summary

Business Identity: "The Implementation Strategist" Positioning: An expert-led, high-margin strategic consultancy that bridges the gap between high-level strategy and technical AI implementation. Model: A "Core/Flex" model, branded as the "Implementation Partnership." The "Core" (Principal) leads all strategy, supported by a partner-provided, certified "Delivery Lead" (secured via a "Practice Retainer") for execution management. Phase 1 Target Market: Australian Higher Education (HEI) sector & adjacent "friendly" verticals for the initial beta. Year 1 Financial Target: c. $570k Revenue / c. $296k Profit (EBITDA @ 52%).

## 2. Strategic Framework (The Business Model Canvas)

### Customer Segments

- Phase 1A (Q1): "Beta Client" (n=1)
    

- A "patient, high-pain" commercial client (HEI or adjacent) secured via direct network. Must be referenceable.
    

- Phase 1B (Q2-Q4): Australian Higher Education Institutions (HEIs)
    

- Initial Target: Private universities or public universities with known, ring-fenced "Transformation Funds."
    
- Economic Buyer: COO, CFO, Chief Digital Officer, Registrar.
    

- Phase 2 (Year 2+): Adjacent Verticals
    

- Healthcare Administration, Logistics, and Financial Services (Compliance/Ops).
    

### Value Propositions

- To Client: "From Boardroom to Working AI in 90 Days." We provide an end-to-end, fixed-price service that designs and delivers a working, high-ROI AI solution to your #1 operational problem.
    
- "Client Success Guarantee": We contractually guarantee that the pilot will achieve the pre-defined KPIs (set in the $45k Roadmap). If it fails, we extend support for 30 days at no charge.
    
- Differentiator:
    

1. Expert-Led: KPMG-level strategy + Westpac-level implementation experience.
    
2. Internal IP: A proprietary "Consultancy OS" (Methodology + SaaS). We use a documented, repeatable methodology combined with best-in-class SaaS tools to ensure speed and quality.
    

### Channels

1. Phase 1A (Primary): Direct Network. Used to secure the single, critical "Beta Client."
    
2. Phase 1B (Primary): Strategic Partnership with "Org." "Org" acts as the trusted, warm channel partner for the HEI sector, governed by performance-based exit clauses.
    
3. Phase 1C (Secondary): Accelerated Thought Leadership. Starting Month 4, using the "Beta Client" case study to build an independent, inbound pipeline via LinkedIn and guest articles.
    

### Customer Relationships

- Model: High-touch, expert-led, trusted advisor. The Principal (Core) owns the C-level relationship; the "Delivery Lead" manages day-to-day execution.
    
- Phased Funnel:
    

1. Acquire (Beta): $75k "Beta Pilot" (for the first client only).
    
2. Acquire (Standard): $15k "AI Strategy Workshop" (100% credited to...)
    
3. Activate: $45k "AI Transformation Roadmap."
    
4. Deliver (3-Tier Model): $150k (MVP) / $195k (Recommended) / $250k+ (Comprehensive) "90-Day Pilot."
    
5. Retain: $300k+ "Annual Scale Program."
    

### Revenue Streams

- 100% Fixed-Price, Project-Based Revenue.
    
- Payment Terms: 100% upfront for <$50k projects. 50% upfront / 30% UAT / 20% Go-Live for pilots. Net 7 terms with 2% late fee. 5% discount for 100% upfront pilot payment.
    
- Product 1 (Beta): $75,000 "Beta Pilot"
    
- Product 2 (Filler): $15,000 "AI Strategy Workshop"
    
- Product 3 (Trust Bridge): $45,000 "AI Transformation Roadmap"
    
- Product 4 (Wedge): $150k - $250k+ "90-Day Pilot" (3-Tier Pricing)
    

### Key Partners

1. "Org" (Channel Partner): Provides HEI leads. Governed by a 6-month performance clause (e.g., 2 qualified $45k leads) to mitigate dependency.
    
2. "Primary" Implementation Partner (Delivery Arm): Vetted, white-label dev shop. Provides the certified "Delivery Lead" (via retainer) and core development team.
    
3. "Bench" Implementation Partner (Redundancy): Secondary vetted partner.
    
4. "Backup Strategist" (Key-Person Mitigation): Paid, retained strategist trained on the "OS" methodology for emergency backup.
    

### Key Activities

1. Service Delivery (60%):
    

- Strategic Governance (30%): Principal's time (Client-facing strategy, solution architecture, QA).
    
- Partner Management (30%): Principal's time (Directing the "Delivery Lead" and partners).
    

2. Business Development (25%): Securing Beta Client, managing "Org," writing thought leadership.
    
3. IP Development (15%): Obsessively documenting the "Beta Pilot" process to build the first "Implementation Playbook" and refine the "Consultancy OS" methodology.
    

### Key Resources

1. Principal Strategist (You): The "Core" expert.
    
2. The "Implementation Partnership": The "Flex" network, including the certified "Delivery Leads."
    
3. "Consultancy OS" (The Moat): The proprietary methodology for combining best-in-class SaaS tools (agent frameworks, process miners) with your documented "Implementation Playbooks."
    
4. Legal/Brand Assets: Trademark for "The Implementation Strategist." Robust contracts (IP Assignment, SOWs, "Pause Clause", "Success Guarantee").
    

### Cost Structure

- Variable Costs (COGS):
    

- "Flex" Partner Fees (Dev): c. 35% of pilot project revenue. (e.g., $52.5k fee on a $150k pilot).
    
- "OS" R&D Fund: 10% of all revenue, earmarked for SaaS licenses and methodology documentation.
    

- Fixed Costs (OPEX):
    

- Insurance: (PI, Cyber, E&O @ $5M+): c. $12,000
    
- Accounting & Legal: c. $8,000
    
- "Practice Retainer" (Primary Delivery Lead): c. $30,000 ($2.5k/month)
    
- "Backup Strategist" Retainer: c. $12,000 ($1k/month)
    
- Trademark/IP Filing: c. $3,000
    
- Software Stack: c. $3,000
    
- Website & Marketing: c. $2,000
    
- Total Annual OPEX: c. $70,000

## 5. Key Risks & Mitigations (V5.2)

|Risk|Mitigation|
|---|---|
|1. Key-Person Risk|"Delivery Lead" Model: Shifts Principal from "delivery" to "governance." "Backup Strategist" Retainer: Paid, trained, contractual backup for emergency.|
|2. Beta Client Failure|Strategic Selection: Target a "patient, high-pain" commercial client (not a non-profit) who agrees to be a case study in return for the $75k beta price.|
|3. Channel Dependency ("Org")|Performance-Based Exit Clause: (e.g., 2x $45k leads in 6mo). Accelerated Parallel GTM: Independent thought-leadership pipeline starts Month 4.|
|4. Partner Quality Failure|"Practice Retainer": Secures the "Delivery Lead" and aligns partner to my practice, not just one project. "Delivery Lead Certification": Mandatory methodology training. <G> "Primary/Bench" Model: Provides delivery redundancy.|
|5. Client Purchase Friction|"Client Success Guarantee": A 30-day "KPI-achieved" guarantee is offered on all pilots, de-risking the client's investment and forcing internal quality.|
|6. Scope Creep / Cash Flow|3-Tier Pricing Model: (MVP/Recommended/Comprehensive) converts scope creep into a pre-priced upsell. Aggressive Payment Terms: 50-100% upfront.|

## 6. Initial 90-Day Implementation Sequence

- Days 1-30:
    

1. Legal & Brand: File for Trademark ("Implementation Strategist"). Bind Insurance policies (PI, Cyber, E&O).
    
2. Partner: Negotiate and sign "Practice Retainer" with Primary Partner.
    
3. Sales: Intensively focus on direct network outreach to identify 3-5 potential Beta Clients.
    

- Days 31-60:
    

1. Sales: Select and sign the Beta Client.
    
2. Onboarding: Conduct "Delivery Lead Certification" with Primary Partner's Lead. Finalize "Org" partnership agreement.
    
3. IP: Begin framework for methodology documentation (the "OS").
    

- Days 61-90:
    

1. Delivery: Kick-off Beta Client project.
    
2. IP: Actively document Beta project methodology.
    
3. Onboarding: Identify and begin onboarding "Backup Strategist."
    
4. Marketing: Establish LinkedIn presence in preparation for Month 4 content launch.
    

- Day 91 Checkpoint:
    

- Beta project is ~30% complete with a tested methodology.
    
- Partners are certified and performing.
    
- "Org" relationship is formalized.
    
- The model is validated and ready for scaling.
