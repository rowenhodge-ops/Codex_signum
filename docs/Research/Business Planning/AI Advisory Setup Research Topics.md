This is the correct next step. The V5.2 plan is a robust set of hypotheses. This research plan is designed to pressure-test and validate (or invalidate) its core assumptions before you commit significant capital or time.

The research is divided into five key risk areas identified in the plan.

### 1. Market & Channel Risk (HEI & "Org" Validation)

This validates the core GTM assumption: that the HEI sector is a viable, accessible, and profitable entry market via the "Org" partnership.

- HEI Procurement & Budgeting Analysis:
    

- Deep Dive: How do Australian universities actually procure $50k - $250k digital transformation and strategic AI projects?
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- What is the role of pre-approved vendor panels (e.g., is "Org" on them)?
    
- What are the typical budget cycles and decision timelines (from first call to SOW signed)?
    
- Who holds the discretionary budget for "operational" (non-academic) innovation? (e.g., COO, CFO, Registrar, Chief Digital Officer).
    

- "Org" Channel Partner Due Diligence (Primary Research):
    

- Deep Dive: Validate "Org's" actual market position, capability, and incentive to sell your high-margin services.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- What is their brand reputation within the HEI sector? Are they seen as strategic partners or just body-shop suppliers/managed service providers?
    
- What is the incentive structure for an "Org" salesperson? Will they get paid more for selling your $45k roadmap (on a 40% cut) than selling $100k of their own internal resources?
    
- Interview 1-2 non-competing partners of "Org" (if possible) to assess their reliability as a channel.
    

- HEI Operational Pain-Point Validation:
    

- Deep Dive: Identify the top 3-5 acute, high-cost, non-academic operational bottlenecks that are "AI-ready."
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Analyze public HEI annual reports and strategic plans for stated goals around "student experience," "operational efficiency," or "digital transformation."
    
- Conduct 3-5 informal "problem discovery" interviews with contacts in university administration (Admissions, Finance, Registrar's Office).
    

### 2. Partner & Ecosystem Risk (The "Practice" Model)

This validates the core operational assumption: that the "Practice Retainer" and "Delivery Lead" model is economically viable and attractive to high-quality partners.

- Partner Economic Model Validation (Primary Research):
    

- Deep Dive: Test the commercial viability of your "Implementation Partnership" offer.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Identify and shortlist 10 high-quality, boutique Australian dev shops (your "Primary Partner" candidates).
    
- Pitch 3-5 of them the exact model: "A $2.5k/mo 'Practice Retainer' for 20% of a senior 'Delivery Lead' + a 30-40% cut of per-project dev fees."
    
- Key questions: Is this compelling? What is their counter-offer? Can they provide a resource who meets your KPMG-level "Delivery Lead" standard?
    

- Talent Mapping & Certification (Backup & Lead):
    

- Deep Dive: Validate the existence and cost of the "Backup Strategist" and "Delivery Lead" talent pool.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Identify 10-15 individuals on LinkedIn (e.g., ex-Big 4 Managers, senior digital PMs) who fit the profile.
    
- What is their market day rate? Is a $1k-$2.5k/mo retainer for ~1-2 days of availability/certification economically viable for them?
    
- Draft the "Delivery Lead Certification" requirements. What are the non-negotiable hard/soft skills?
    

### 3. Product & Pricing Risk (The Phased Offering)

This validates the core product assumption: that clients will pay a premium for your specific, phased, and guaranteed offering.

- "Paid-Discovery" Market Validation:
    

- Deep Dive: Determine if the $45k "AI Transformation Roadmap" is a viable, standalone product or a "free" sales tool in the current market.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Analyze how competitors (Big 4, Slalom, ThoughtWorks, AI boutiques) price their "discovery" or "diagnostic" phases.
    
- Is the "Paid Discovery" model common in the Australian market, or will it be a significant hurdle?
    

- "Success Guarantee" Competitive Analysis:
    

- Deep Dive: Assess the actual market value and uniqueness of your "Client Success Guarantee."
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Are competitors already offering this (explicitly or implicitly)?
    
- Draft the contractual language for the guarantee. What are the exact, measurable "KPIs" you would be willing to guarantee (e.g., "process time reduction," "manual-entry elimination")?
    

### 4. Competitive & IP Risk (The "Moat")

This validates the strategic assumption: that your positioning and methodology are a durable competitive advantage.

- "Implementation Strategist" Market Gap Analysis:
    

- Deep Dive: Confirm that the "strategy-to-implementation" niche is actually unoccupied.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Map the top 15 competitors. Who truly owns this space?
    
- Analyze the marketing language of AI boutiques (e.g., Amperfii, Eliiza) and mid-tier firms (e.g., Slalom). Are they already selling this "end-to-end" model?
    

- "OS as Methodology" Durability:
    

- Deep Dive: Assess the commoditization risk of your IP.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- What is the specific, best-in-class SaaS stack you would use for the "OS"? (e.g., OpenAI/Azure agent frameworks, Celonis/UiPath for process mining, specific low-code platforms).
    
- How quickly are these tools commoditizing the methodology itself? (i.e., making it easy for any consultant to do what you do).
    

### 5. Beta Client Risk

This validates the immediate tactical assumption: that you can find a beta client who provides a transferable case study.

- Beta Client Prospectus & Profile:
    

- Deep Dive: Develop the "Beta Client" persona and offer.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- Define the "patient, high-pain" client profile. What is their ideal title, industry, and specific business pain?
    
- Create the 1-page "Beta Partnership" offer ($75k for a $195k value pilot).
    

- Case Study Transferability Assessment:
    

- Deep Dive: Validate that a non-HEI beta project will be relevant to the HEI market.
    
- Sub-topics:
    

- If your beta is a bank's "KYC Onboarding," how directly does that process map to an HEI's "Student Admissions"?
    
- Draft the "sanitized" case study. Can you write it in a generic "Horizontal Process" (e.g., "Automating Document-Heavy Compliance Workflows") language that appeals to both?
