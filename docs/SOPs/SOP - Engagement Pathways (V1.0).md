# The Gravity Well

### 1.0 Objective & Strategic Link

Objective: To define the high-probability engagement flows (C1-C4) that generate inbound C4: Economic Buyer meetings, satisfying the strategic goal: "I want people to come to me."

Strategic Link: This SOP is the "master" workflow for the 90-day "Cold Start."

1. It is fueled by the SOP - Market Intel V1.0 (which generates the "Killer Question").
    
2. It executes via the SOP - Stakeholder Engagement V1.0 (which defines the scripts/tools).
    
3. Its sole purpose is to avoid "peddling" by creating a "warm gravitational pull" around a C4 target.
    

### 2.0 The "Gravity Model": Two Phases of Engagement

#### Phase 1: Proactive "Gravity Generation" (Days 1-90)

This is your only focus during the "Cold Start" (per Business Plan). You are not "selling." You are "building gravity" by executing your SOPs on C1, C2, and C3 contacts.

#### Phase 2: Reactive "Inbound Pull" (Year 1+)

This is the reward for Phase 1. This is when "people come to you." This phase is earned and has two primary channels:

- Channel A: The Codex Index (Automated Proof): A stranger (a future C4) sees your dashboard of validated, anonymous ROI (from the Colophon) and contacts you.
    
- Channel B: Word-of-Mouth (Earned Proof): A former client (C4) or contact (C1/C3) refers you.
    

This SOP focuses only on the Phase 1 pathways required to earn Phase 2.

### 3.0 Phase 1: High-Probability Engagement Pathways

This is the flow. Never jump the flow. A "warm intro" is the only acceptable path to a C4 in this model.

#### Pathway 1: The "C1 Opener" (Highest-Status Path)

This path leverages pure, "peer-level" authority.

1. Start (C1): You engage a C1: Strategic Navigator (e.g., Monash HBR Prof).
    
2. Forum: "Strategic Review Session" (per SOP - Stakeholder Engagement).
    
3. Action: You "Red Team" your model. You impress them with your systemic thinking (the "powerhouse of automation" V5.3 plan).
    
4. The "Ask": "Based on this model, who is the one COO in your network who you think would 'get' this?"
    
5. Result (The "Pull"): The C1 provides a high-status, peer-to-peer email introduction to a C4: Economic Buyer.
    
6. Probability: Very High. The intro is the validation.
    

#### Pathway 2: The "C3 Bridge" (Intel-Led Path)

This is the "business architect's" path. It leverages your "Killer Question."

1. Start (C3): You engage a C3: Market Intel contact (e.g., UoW Snr. Mgr).
    
2. Prep: You must first complete the SOP - Market Intel V1.0 for their organization.
    
3. Forum: "Market Intelligence Call."
    
4. Action: You use your "Killer Question" (e.g., "I see T1 broke your workload mapping..."). You prove you know more about their "messy problem" than any other consultant.
    
5. The "Ask": "This is the exact 'business architecture' problem I solve. Who is the operational lead (C4) responsible for fixing this?"
    
6. Result (The "Pull"): The C3—impressed by your homework—provides a problem-led, "savior-in-waiting" introduction to the C4 (e.g., "I just spoke to an expert who understands our T1 problem...").
    
7. Probability: High. The intro is a validated solution to a known problem.
    

#### Pathway 3: The "C2 Flank" (Partner-Led Path)

This path leverages a shared interest.

1. Start (C2): You engage a C2: Peer Collaborator (e.g., UoW AI Guy).
    
2. Forum: "Partnership Sync."
    
3. Action: You find the "gap" between your "business architecture" skills and their "technical" skills. You define a joint offering.
    
4. The "Ask": "Let's pitch this joint solution to a C4 you have access to. I'll handle the 'architecture,' you handle the 'AI'."
    
5. Result (The "Pull"): The C2 provides a capability-led, "team" introduction to a C4.
    
6. Probability: Medium. Relies on the C2's credibility, but is a strong "flanking" move.
    

#### Pathway 0: The "Cold Pitch" (The "Peddling" Trap)

This path is a violation of the entire model.

1. Start (You): You find a C4 on LinkedIn.
    
2. Action: You send a cold email "peddling" your $15k Initium.
    
3. Result: You are ignored (per your own logic). You have no "gravity," no social proof, and no validated intel. You have devalued your "Front Stage" brand.
    
4. Probability: Very Low. (FORBIDDEN IN THIS MODEL)
    

### 4.0 Tools & Mediums for "Gravity Generation"

- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (The "Map"): This is your "gravity-mapper." You never use it to "InMail" a C4. You only use it to identify your target C4 and then map the C1, C2, and C3 contacts in their orbit that you have a 1st or 2nd-degree connection to.
    
- Email / Warm Intro (The "Path"): This is the only medium for first contact. It must be a warm introduction from your "six-degrees" SOP (Path 1, 2, or 3).
    
- Obsidian (The "Intel"): This is where you log the intel from SOP - Market Intel before the call, and where you commit the new intel after the call (per SOP - Stakeholder Engagement).
    
- Google Meet (The "Forum"): This is the "Front Stage." It's where you execute the call, armed with your "Killer Question," to generate the gravity.
