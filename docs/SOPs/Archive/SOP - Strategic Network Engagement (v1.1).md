## 1.0 Objective

To systematically gather market intelligence, validate the core value proposition ("holistic strategist"), and secure warm introductions to operational leaders. This SOP governs all initial network engagement before a final product offering is defined. Its primary goal is intelligence gathering and validation, not direct sales.

## 2.0 Scope

This SOP applies to all initial outreach and inbound inquiries from the Principal's professional network. It is suspended for a specific contact only when that contact is qualified as a high-intent "Economic Buyer" (at which point, a formal Sales SOP takes over).

## 3.0 Procedure

### Phase 1: Contact Triage & Categorization

Before any outreach, every new contact (inbound or outbound) must be triaged and assigned one of the following categories. This categorization defines the goal, focus, and desired outcome of the meeting.

|Category|Description|Primary Goal|Focus (Your Value Prop Anchor)|Key Risk to Manage|Desired Outcome (Of the Engagement)|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|C1: Strategic Navigator|High-status thinkers, academics, or senior leaders (e.g., Monash HBR Prof). They operate at a 30,000-foot view.|Red Team the Premise. Gain high-level market validation and, potentially, a high-status advocate.|Testing the 'Big Idea' (The Provocation). Your value prop to them is an interesting, high-level strategic challenge.|Wasting their time with a generic "get to know you" chat.|A "red-teamed" and validated strategic premise. OR a high-status advocate who may make future C-level intros.|
|C2: Peer Collaborator|People who do part of what you do. (e.g., UoW AI Consultant, Infosys Devs). They are potential partners or competitors.|Find the "Gaps." Identify opportunities for a Channel (they send you clients) or Partner (you use them for delivery) relationship.|Partnership & Gaps (What do they hate doing?). ### Phase 2: Engagement & Scripting

1. Log Contact: Add the contact to the Contact Tracker (see 4.0 Tools) and assign their C1-C3 category.
    
2. Select Script: Use the approved script framework for that category, informed by the Focus column.
    

- For C1 (Navigator): The "Strategic Provocation" Ask.
    

- Script: "I'm challenging [big-corp premise] with [my new premise]. From your seat at [HBR/Monash], what part of my hypothesis is wrong?"
    

- For C2 (Collaborator): The "Partner Gap" Ask.
    

- Script: "I'm a tool-agnostic strategist. What are the messy strategy/people/process problems that clients bring you that you don't want to solve?"
    

- For C3 (Market Intel): The "Honest Ask" (Intel Gathering).
    

- Script: "This is not a sales call. I'm in my initial validation phase and I'm hoping to borrow your brain to understand the real operational challenges... "
    

3. Execute Outreach: Send the message via LinkedIn or email.
    
4. Update Tracker: Log the outreach as "Contacted - Awaiting Reply."
    

### Phase 3: Conversation Execution

1. Frame the Call: Begin every C1-C3 call by re-stating the non-sales frame.
    

- Script: "As promised, this isn't a sales call. My only goal is to [state goal from Phase 1, e.g., 'get your advice', 'find the gaps', 'hear your perspective']."
    

2. Execute the Game Plan:
    

- C1: Present your 5-min "provocation" and then stop talking. Let them "red team" it.
    
- C2: Listen for what they hate doing (e.g., stakeholder management, process mapping). This is your "wedge."
    
- C3: Ask discovery questions. "What's the #1 operational fire...?" "What's the 'messy' project that...?"
    

3. The "Specific Ask": At the end of the call, make one specific request based on the conversation, aiming for your Desired Outcome.
    

- Script: "This has been incredibly helpful. That problem you mentioned about [re-state their messy problem] is exactly what I'm built for. Who is the one person in your network who is most focused on solving that right now?"
    

### Phase 4: Post-Call Processing & Intel Logging

This is the most critical step. A call that does not generate logged intel is a failure.

1. Immediate Follow-up: Send a thank-you note within 1 hour, referencing a key insight they shared.
    
2. Log Intel: Open the Contact Tracker and your Obsidian vault.
    
3. Intel Extraction (Mandatory):
    

- Key Language: Log the exact words and phrases the contact used (e.g., "drowning in manual paperwork," "data is a nightmare," "no internal resources").
    
- "Messy Problems": Summarize the 1-3 specific operational problems they described.
    
- Introductions: Log any new contacts they offered to introduce.
    

4. Update Status: Update the Contact Tracker status to "Conversation Held" and log the "Next Action" (e.g., "Follow up on intro to X," "Add to 3-month nurture").
    

## 4.0 Tools & Resources

1. Contact Tracker: A simple spreadsheet/database with the following columns:
    

- Name
    
- Organisation
    
- Category (C1-C4)
    
- Status (Pending, Contacted, Meeting Set, Held, Nurture)
    
- Last Contact Date
    
- Next Action
    
- Key Intel (Link to Obsidian note)
    

2. Obsidian (or PKM): The central repository for all "Key Language" and "Messy Problem" notes, tagged by contact.
    
3. LinkedIn Sales Navigator: For mapping contacts and identifying new ones based on patterns.
    

## 5.0 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Leading KPI: # of C1-C3 Conversations Held (Target: 10-15)
    
- Success KPI: # of Warm Introductions Received (This is the #1 signal of success)
    
- Strategic KPI: # of "Messy Problem" Patterns Identified (This becomes your $15k diagnostic)
