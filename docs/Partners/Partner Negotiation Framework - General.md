# Partner Negotiation Framework - General

**Date**: 09-Nov-2025  
**Status**: Active Framework  
**Owner**: Principal  
**Purpose**: Master template for evaluating and structuring strategic partnerships

---

## Executive Summary

This framework governs all partnership negotiations for Codex Signum. It protects core business assets (client relationships, IP, methodology) while enabling strategic collaboration where mutual value exists.

**Core Principle**: I negotiate from strength. Partnerships must enhance business value without compromising independence, methodology ownership, or client relationships.

**Framework Application**: Use this template for any potential partner category:

- Technical delivery partners (implementation specialists)
- Channel partners (referral sources)
- Strategic alliances (joint go-to-market)
- Technology vendors (platform partnerships)

---

## Partnership Evaluation Criteria

Before entering negotiations, assess potential partner against these criteria:

### 1. Strategic Fit Assessment

**Questions to Answer**:

- What specific capability or market access does this partner provide that I lack?
- Does this partnership accelerate my business model or distract from it?
- Can I achieve the same outcome independently in 6-12 months?
- Does this partner respect fixed-price, outcome-based business models?

**Scoring** (1-5 scale):

- **5**: Critical capability I cannot build/access independently
- **4**: Significant acceleration of market entry or delivery capability
- **3**: Moderate value but achievable alternatives exist
- **2**: Marginal value, easily replaced
- **1**: No clear strategic value

**Minimum Score to Proceed**: 3/5

---

### 2. Economic Viability Assessment

**Questions to Answer**:

- Does the revenue split preserve my target margins (50%+ gross margin minimum)?
- Are transaction costs justified by deal size or strategic value?
- Can I maintain price control and avoid margin erosion?
- Does partnership enable scalability or create new dependencies?

**Example Margin Analysis**:

```
Engagement Value: $150,000
Partner Share: $60,000 (40%)
My Share: $90,000 (60%)
My COGS: $20,000
My Gross Profit: $70,000
My Margin: 78% ✓ (exceeds 50% minimum)
```

**Minimum Acceptable Margins**:

- Advisory work I deliver: 85%+ gross margin
- Implementation work with partner: 50%+ gross margin
- Referral arrangements: 70%+ gross margin after fee

**Walk Away If**: Partnership reduces gross margin below 50% on any engagement type

---

### 3. Relationship Control Assessment

**Questions to Answer**:

- Who owns the client relationship post-engagement?
- Can partner contact my clients without my approval?
- Do I maintain contract and billing control?
- Can I exit partnership without losing client access?

**Control Hierarchy** (ranked by importance):

1. **Client relationship ownership** (non-negotiable: I own clients I originate)
2. **Contract control** (I must be prime contractor on my clients)
3. **Communication control** (partner requires my approval for client contact)
4. **Exit rights** (I can terminate with defined notice period)

**Walk Away If**: Partner demands ownership of relationships I originate

---

### 4. IP & Methodology Protection Assessment

**Questions to Answer**:

- Does partner request access to my proprietary systems (Codex, Colophon)?
- Will deliverables expose my methodology to replication risk?
- Can I protect competitive advantage while collaborating?
- Does partner have competing methodology they might claim conflicts?

**IP Protection Tiers**:

- **Tier 1 - Never Share**: Codex architecture, Colophon process design, automation systems, AI-native operational framework
- **Tier 2 - Limited Access**: Client-specific deliverables for joint projects (reports, presentations)
- **Tier 3 - Market Materials**: Public-facing content, case studies (sanitized), general capability statements

**Walk Away If**: Partner demands Tier 1 access or claims co-ownership of methodology

---

### 5. Brand & Market Position Assessment

**Questions to Answer**:

- How will this partnership affect my market positioning?
- Will I be perceived as independent strategic advisor or partner's subcontractor?
- Can I build MY brand through this partnership?
- Does partner have reputation risks that transfer to me?

**Brand Impact Scenarios**:

- **Positive**: Partner introduces me to C-suite forums, co-presents as peer, references my methodology
- **Neutral**: Partner provides delivery capability behind scenes, I remain client-facing
- **Negative**: Partner positions me as their resource, diminishes my independence

**Walk Away If**: Partnership damages my positioning as tool-agnostic strategic advisor

## Universal Non-Negotiables

These rules apply to ALL partnerships, regardless of type or structure:

### Rule 1: Client Relationship Ownership

**My Position**:

> "Any client relationship I originate—through my network, my marketing, or my business development—remains mine in perpetuity. Partners may participate in delivery or receive referral fees, but I own the client relationship, contract, and billing control."

**Examples of MY Originated Relationships**:

- Contacts from my professional network (Herman Tse, UoW connections)
- Inbound leads from my marketing or content
- Referrals from my existing clients
- Relationships I've invested time building (C1/C2/C3 engagement per my SOP)

**What This Means**:

- I am the prime contractor on all engagements
- Partner cannot contact client without my explicit approval
- All client communications flow through me or with my coordination
- If partnership ends, client relationship stays with me

**Walk-Away Trigger**: Partner demands ownership of clients I originate

---

### Rule 2: Revenue Share Must Preserve Margins

**My Position**:

> "All partnership revenue splits must preserve minimum 50% gross margin on my share. I will not accept structures that reduce my economics below this threshold regardless of strategic value claimed."

**Minimum Margin Requirements by Engagement Type**:

| Engagement Type                                | My Target Margin | Maximum Partner Share        |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Advisory work I deliver (Initium/Consilium)    | 85%+             | 15% max (if partner sourced) |
| Implementation with partner delivery (Fabrica) | 50%+             | 50% max                      |
| Ongoing support/governance (Vigilia)           | 65%+             | 35% max                      |

**Calculation Method**:

```
My Gross Margin = (My Revenue - My COGS) / My Revenue

Example:
Engagement: $150,000 total
Partner Share: $60,000 (40%)
My Share: $90,000 (60%)
My COGS: $20,000
My Gross Margin: ($90k - $20k) / $90k = 77.8% ✓
```

**Walk-Away Trigger**: Proposed split reduces my gross margin below 50%

---

### Rule 3: Intellectual Property Protection

**My Position**:

> "My diagnostic frameworks, knowledge systems (Codex), validation processes (Colophon), and operational methodology remain my proprietary intellectual property. Partners receive no license, access, or co-ownership rights to these systems."

**Protected Assets**:

- **Codex**: Knowledge architecture, data models, AI integration patterns
- **Colophon**: ROI validation framework, measurement systems, feedback loops
- **Initium Framework**: Diagnostic methodology, assessment templates, analysis patterns
- **Consilium Framework**: Roadmap methodology, stakeholder engagement protocols
- **Fabrica Delivery Patterns**: Implementation frameworks, project management systems
- **AI-Native Operations**: Automation architecture, system integration patterns

**Shareable Assets** (with restrictions):

- Client-specific deliverables (reports, presentations) for joint engagements
- Sanitized case studies for marketing (client-approved)
- General capability statements and service descriptions

**Walk-Away Trigger**: Partner requests access to methodology, claims co-ownership, or attempts to replicate systems

---

### Rule 4: Independence & Tool Neutrality

**My Position**:

> "I maintain tool-agnostic positioning and independence to select the best solution for each client. I will not accept exclusive partnerships that limit my ability to choose delivery partners, technology platforms, or methodologies based on client needs."

**What This Protects**:

- My reputation as independent strategic advisor (not vendor-aligned)
- My ability to negotiate best pricing/terms across multiple partners
- My flexibility to pivot if partner quality/pricing deteriorates
- My credibility when advising on technology selection

**Acceptable**: "Preferred partner for X capability with right of first refusal"  
**Unacceptable**: "Exclusive delivery partner for all engagements"

**Walk-Away Trigger**: Partner demands exclusivity or restricts my partner selection freedom

---

### Rule 5: Defined Exit Terms

**My Position**:

> "All partnerships must include clear exit terms with defined notice periods, client transition protocols, and IP ownership clarification. Either party can exit for any reason with appropriate notice."

**Minimum Exit Terms**:

- **Notice Period**: 30-90 days depending on engagement duration
- **Client Transition**: I retain all client relationships; partner has no contact rights post-exit
- **Work-in-Progress**: Clear rules for completing or transitioning active engagements
- **IP Ownership**: Confirmed that all methodology, systems, client deliverables remain mine
- **Non-Compete**: Reasonable restrictions (e.g., partner can't directly solicit my clients for 12 months)

**Walk-Away Trigger**: Partner resists exit terms or creates lock-in dependencies

## Partnership Structure Options

Choose structure based on partner capability and strategic fit:

### Option A: Strategic Alliance

**When to Use**: Partner provides complementary capability for joint go-to-market

**Structure**:

- Non-exclusive partnership
- Defined market segments or client types
- Revenue splits by client origination source
- Joint marketing and brand building
- Pilot period (6-12 months) with quarterly reviews

**Example Use Case**:

- Partner has existing client base in target sector (e.g., healthcare, education)
- I provide strategic methodology layer
- Partner provides technical delivery capability
- Both parties contribute to business development

**Revenue Split Framework**:

| Client Source       | Advisory (Initium/Consilium) | Implementation (Fabrica) | Governance (Vigilia) |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------ | -------------------- |
| My origination      | 100% me                      | 60% me / 40% partner     | 70% me / 30% partner |
| Partner origination | 70% me / 30% partner         | 50% me / 50% partner     | 50% me / 50% partner |
| Joint origination   | 60% me / 40% partner         | 50% me / 50% partner     | 50% me / 50% partner |

**Success Metrics**:

- Number of joint engagements closed
- Revenue per partner (target: $200k+ annually each)
- Client satisfaction scores (9/10 average minimum)
- Conversion rates (Initium → Fabrica target: 40%+)

---

### Option B: Delivery Partner (Subcontractor)

**When to Use**: I need specialized technical capability for implementation phase

**Structure**:

- I am prime contractor, partner is subcontractor
- Partner delivers defined scope under my direction
- I maintain all client communication and relationship management
- Partner paid on milestone or completion basis

**Example Use Case**:

- I've sold Fabrica engagement
- Need specialized technical skills (e.g., RPA development, AI implementation, cloud architecture)
- Partner executes technical scope I've defined in Consilium phase
- I remain strategic product owner and client-facing lead

**Revenue Split Framework**:

| My Role                                    | Partner Role                | My Share | Partner Share |
| ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------- | -------- | ------------- |
| Strategy + product ownership + client mgmt | Technical delivery          | 50-60%   | 40-50%        |
| Full engagement delivery                   | Specialized technical tasks | 70-80%   | 20-30%        |

**Partner Compensation**:

- Fixed price for defined scope (no time-and-materials)
- Payment on milestones: 40% on start, 30% mid-project, 30% on completion
- Paid Net 30 after I receive client payment
- Change orders require my approval with client before execution

**Key Terms**:

- Partner must use my project management framework
- All client deliverables reviewed and approved by me before delivery
- Partner represents themselves as working "with" me, not independently
- Non-solicitation: Partner cannot approach my clients for 24 months post-engagement

---

### Option C: Referral/Channel Partner

**When to Use**: Partner can provide qualified leads but has no delivery role

**Structure**:

- Partner refers opportunities from their network
- I deliver independently (with my chosen delivery partners)
- Partner receives one-time referral fee on closed business only
- No ongoing relationship management by partner after introduction

**Example Use Case**:

- Consultant, coach, or advisor with client relationships in my target market
- They identify transformation needs but don't deliver this work
- They introduce me, I qualify and close
- They receive fee for successful introduction

**Referral Fee Framework**:

| Engagement Type                  | Total Value | Referral Fee             | My Net Revenue    |
| -------------------------------- | ----------- | ------------------------ | ----------------- |
| Initium only                     | $15,000     | 10-15% ($1,500-$2,250)   | $12,750-$13,500   |
| Initium + Consilium              | $45,000     | 10-15% ($4,500-$6,750)   | $38,250-$40,500   |
| Full journey (Init+Cons+Fabrica) | $195,000    | 10-15% ($19,500-$29,250) | $165,750-$175,500 |

**Key Terms**:

- Fee paid only on signed contracts (not proposals or conversations)
- One-time fee per client relationship (not recurring on renewals/expansions)
- 90-day protection period: If client doesn't sign within 90 days of introduction, referral expires
- Partner provides quarterly reporting on referred opportunities
- I provide status updates on referred client progress

**When Referral Fee Applies**:

- Partner made direct introduction with warm context
- Client signed contract within 90 days of introduction
- This was a NEW relationship (not existing contact)

**When Referral Fee Does NOT Apply**:

- I already knew the client/prospect
- Introduction was "cold" (just forwarded a name without context)
- Client didn't sign within 90-day window
- Partner's involvement was minimal or coincidental

---

### Option D: Technology/Platform Partnership

**When to Use**: Software vendor or platform provider wants to integrate or co-sell

**Structure**:

- I remain technology-agnostic in client recommendations
- Partner provides preferential pricing or technical support
- Potential co-marketing for specific use cases
- No exclusivity or lock-in

**Example Use Case**:

- Automation platform (e.g., UiPath, Zapier) wants consulting partner
- AI platform (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic) offers partner program
- Cloud provider (e.g., AWS, Google Cloud) has consulting partner tier

**Value Exchange**:

- **Partner Provides**: Technical support, preferential pricing, co-marketing opportunities, sales leads
- **I Provide**: Client implementations, case studies, feedback on product roadmap

**Revenue Models**:

- **Implementation fees**: I charge client for implementation, may buy partner licenses at discount
- **Referral revenue share**: Partner pays commission if I influence technology selection (10-20% of annual contract value)
- **Support retainers**: Partner pays me to provide implementation/support services to their clients

**Key Terms**:

- Non-exclusive: I maintain freedom to recommend competing solutions
- No reseller markup: If I resell partner technology, minimal margin (5-10% admin fee)
- Client ownership: Clients are mine, not transferred to partner
- Independence preservation: I advise based on client needs, not partner incentives

## Negotiation Process & Tactics

### Phase 1: Pre-Negotiation Intelligence (Before First Meeting)

**Research Questions to Answer**:

1. What is partner's current business model? (day rate vs. fixed price vs. product)
2. What is their revenue scale and growth trajectory?
3. Who are their existing partners and what are typical terms?
4. What is their reputation in market? (ask trusted contacts)
5. What is their financial stability? (profitable, VC-backed, struggling?)
6. What is their strategic objective for this partnership? (revenue, capability, market access?)

**Intelligence Sources**:

- LinkedIn (company page, employee backgrounds, content)
- Website (client list, case studies, team size)
- Google search (news, reviews, complaints)
- Network inquiries (ask mutual contacts privately)
- Financial databases (if public or reported)

**Red Flags to Watch For**:

- Financial instability or recent layoffs
- High employee turnover (check LinkedIn tenure)
- Reputation issues or client complaints
- Competing partnerships that conflict
- Aggressive sales tactics or pressure

---

### Phase 2: Initial Discussion (Discovery & Framing)

**My Opening Framework**:

> "Thanks for reaching out. I'm interested in exploring potential alignment, but I want to be transparent about how I operate so we can quickly assess fit.
>
> **My Business Model**: [Describe fixed-price, outcome-based approach]  
> **My Non-Negotiables**: [State client ownership, IP protection, independence]  
> **My Interest**: [Specific capability or market access this partner provides]
>
> Before we discuss commercial terms, help me understand: [Ask 2-3 strategic questions about their business model, partnership approach, client ownership expectations]"

**Goals for First Meeting**:

- Assess strategic fit (use criteria above)
- Identify partner's true motivation and leverage
- Test their understanding of fixed-price vs. day-rate economics
- Surface any deal-breaker expectations early
- Avoid committing to anything; this is discovery only

**Questions to Ask**:

1. "What specific value do you see in partnering with me vs. building this capability internally?"
2. "What's your typical partnership structure and revenue split?"
3. "How do you handle client relationship ownership when working with partners?"
4. "What happens to client relationships if our partnership ends?"
5. "What access or integration would you need with my methodology or systems?"

**Watch For**:

- Vague answers about economics (suggests they're testing your negotiation strength)
- Immediate discussion of exclusivity (red flag)
- Requests for methodology details before commercial terms agreed (IP risk)
- Pressure to commit quickly (suggests desperation or manipulation)

---

### Phase 3: Commercial Terms Discussion

**My Negotiation Approach**:

1. **Anchor High**: Start with most favorable terms for me, let them negotiate down
2. **Package Components**: Bundle relationship ownership + revenue split + exit terms together
3. **Use Silence**: After stating my position, stop talking and let them respond
4. **Never Justify**: State terms as facts, not requests ("This is how I structure partnerships")
5. **Walk-Away Ready**: Demonstrate I don't need this deal

**Commercial Term Negotiation Sequence**:

**Step 1: Establish Client Ownership Rules**

> "Let's start with relationship ownership. Any client I originate through my network or business development remains my client. I'm the prime contractor, I own billing and communication. You participate as a delivery partner or receive a referral fee depending on your role. Non-negotiable."

**Step 2: Propose Revenue Split**

> "Here's how I structure revenue splits: [Present framework from Partnership Structure Options above]. This preserves my minimum margins and compensates you fairly for your contribution. What are your thoughts?"

**Step 3: Define IP Boundaries**

> "My methodology and systems are proprietary. You'll have access to client deliverables for joint projects, but not my internal frameworks. This protects both of us—you have your IP, I have mine."

**Step 4: Propose Pilot Structure**

> "I suggest we pilot this with [2-3] engagements over [6] months. Quarterly reviews, either party can exit with [30-60] day notice. This lets us test the model with minimal risk."

**Counter-Offer Response Framework**:

- **If reasonable**: "I can work with that if we adjust [related term]"
- **If below minimums**: "That doesn't preserve my required margins. I need [my number] to make this viable"
- **If unreasonable**: "I don't think we're aligned on partnership economics. Let me think about whether there's a different structure that works"

---

### Phase 4: Contract Negotiation

**Key Contract Terms Checklist**:

**Commercial Terms**:

- [ ] Revenue split by client origination source clearly defined
- [ ] Payment terms and timing specified (milestone-based preferred)
- [ ] Invoicing and payment method established
- [ ] Change order process defined
- [ ] Minimum/maximum annual commitments (if any)

**Relationship Control**:

- [ ] Client ownership explicitly stated
- [ ] Communication protocols defined (who contacts clients when)
- [ ] Prime contractor designation confirmed
- [ ] Decision-making authority clarified

**IP Protection**:

- [ ] My methodology and systems remain my property
- [ ] Partner methodology and systems remain theirs
- [ ] Joint work product ownership rules defined
- [ ] Confidentiality and non-disclosure terms

**Operational Terms**:

- [ ] Scope of work and deliverables defined
- [ ] Quality standards and acceptance criteria
- [ ] Project management and reporting requirements
- [ ] Issue escalation and resolution process

**Exit & Termination**:

- [ ] Notice period specified (30-90 days)
- [ ] Termination for cause conditions defined
- [ ] Work-in-progress completion or transition rules
- [ ] Post-termination obligations (non-solicit, IP return, etc.)
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanism (mediation, arbitration)

**Insurance & Liability**:

- [ ] Professional indemnity insurance requirements
- [ ] Liability caps and limitations
- [ ] Indemnification for IP infringement
- [ ] Force majeure provisions

**Legal Review**:

- Always have my legal counsel review before signing
- Focus review on: IP protection, client ownership, exit terms
- Negotiate any concerning language before execution
- Don't let partner's urgency pressure me into signing without review

## Common Negotiation Scenarios & Responses

### Scenario 1: Partner Demands Client Ownership

**Their Position**: "We need to own the client relationship since we made the introduction / have existing relationship / are providing the platform"

**My Response**:

> "I understand you value the client relationship. However, I only work under structures where I own relationships I've built or been introduced to. This is fundamental to my business model.
>
> What I can offer: If this is your existing client, I'll work as your subcontractor on a fixed-price basis. You remain prime contractor, I deliver defined scope. Or, you receive a referral fee and I take the relationship forward independently. Your choice, but I don't transfer client ownership."

**If They Insist**: Walk away. Non-negotiable.

---

### Scenario 2: Partner Wants Deep Discount for "Strategic Value"

**Their Position**: "We can bring you significant volume, but we need better pricing/larger share to make this work at scale"

**My Response**:

> "I appreciate the potential volume. However, my minimum margins are not flexible—they're what make my business viable at any scale.
>
> If you need a larger share, we can explore a different structure: [Option 1] You take prime contractor role and pay me a fixed subcontractor fee, or [Option 2] You receive a referral fee and I deliver independently. But I won't reduce my gross margin below 50% regardless of projected volume."

**If They Insist**: Walk away. Economic viability is non-negotiable.

---

### Scenario 3: Partner Requests Methodology Access

**Their Position**: "To deliver effectively, we need access to your diagnostic framework / assessment templates / process methodology"

**My Response**:

> "I protect my methodology as my core competitive advantage. What I can provide:
>
> - Training on how to work within my delivery framework
> - Clear requirements and specifications for your delivery scope
> - Templates for specific client deliverables we're co-creating
>
> What I cannot provide:
>
> - Access to my internal assessment frameworks
> - My knowledge systems or automation platforms
> - Proprietary templates or intellectual property
>
> If you need full methodology access to participate, we're not a fit."

**If They Insist**: Walk away. IP protection is non-negotiable.

---

### Scenario 4: Partner Pushes for Exclusivity

**Their Position**: "We need exclusivity in [sector/geography/capability] to justify the investment in this partnership"

**My Response**:

> "I maintain tool-agnostic positioning to serve my clients' best interests. I can offer:
>
> - 'Preferred partner' status with right of first refusal
> - Commitment to involve you in [X%] of opportunities where your capability fits
> - Co-marketing in specific use cases or sectors
>
> What I cannot offer:
>
> - Exclusive partnership that limits my delivery options
> - Restrictions on my ability to work with competing partners
> - Lock-in that compromises my independence
>
> If exclusivity is required, we should end the discussion here."

**If They Insist**: Walk away. Independence is non-negotiable.

---

### Scenario 5: Partner Wants Long Lock-In Period

**Their Position**: "We need a [2-3 year] minimum commitment to make the investment in onboarding and integration worthwhile"

**My Response**:

> "I understand you want stability. I can offer:
>
> - [6-12 month] pilot with defined success metrics
> - Auto-renewal with [90-day] notice to terminate
> - Commitment to give you right of first refusal during pilot
>
> What I cannot offer:
>
> - Multi-year lock-in without exit clause
> - Minimum revenue commitments beyond pilot phase
> - Penalties for early termination
>
> Let's prove the partnership works in the pilot, then we can discuss longer-term arrangements."

**If They Insist**: Counter with longer pilot (12 months) but maintain exit rights. If they demand true lock-in, walk away.

## Red Flags & Walk-Away Triggers

### Immediate Walk-Away Signals (End Discussion)

🚫 **Partner claims ownership of clients I originate or introduce**

- No negotiation possible; this violates Rule 1

🚫 **Partner demands access to proprietary methodology/systems**

- No negotiation possible; this violates Rule 3

🚫 **Partner insists on economics that drop my margin below 50%**

- No negotiation possible; this violates Rule 2

🚫 **Partner requires exclusive lock-in without exit terms**

- No negotiation possible; this violates Rules 4 & 5

🚫 **Partner has reputation issues or ethical concerns**

- Reputation damage risk too high; walk away

🚫 **Partner uses high-pressure tactics or false scarcity**

- Signals desperation or manipulation; walk away

---

### Warning Signals (Negotiate Carefully or Walk)

⚠️ **Partner is evasive about their business model or financial stability**

- Risk: They may be struggling and seeking to extract value
- Action: Demand transparency or walk away

⚠️ **Partner wants to meet my clients before commercial terms agreed**

- Risk: Attempting to go direct and cut me out
- Action: No client introductions until contract signed

⚠️ **Partner keeps changing terms or introducing new requirements**

- Risk: Moving goal posts; no stable agreement possible
- Action: Set deadline for final terms or walk away

⚠️ **Partner has high turnover or operational instability**

- Risk: Delivery quality issues, client satisfaction risk
- Action: Require stronger performance guarantees or walk away

⚠️ **Partner's "standard terms" all favor them heavily**

- Risk: Unwilling to negotiate fair partnership
- Action: Demand balanced terms or walk away

⚠️ **Partner wants to brand me as their employee/division**

- Risk: Damages my independent advisor positioning
- Action: Demand clear co-branding or walk away

## Post-Agreement Partnership Management

### Pilot Phase Management (First 6-12 Months)

**Monthly Partnership Reviews**:

- Revenue generated by each party
- Engagement quality and client satisfaction
- Operational friction points
- Adherence to agreed commercial terms
- Brand and market positioning impact

**Quarterly Strategic Reviews**:

- Overall partnership ROI assessment
- Refinement of processes and communication
- Discussion of expansion or specialization opportunities
- Decision to continue, modify, or terminate

**Success Metrics to Track**:

- Number of joint engagements closed
- Revenue per partner (absolute and percentage)
- Gross margin achievement vs. targets
- Client satisfaction scores
- Delivery quality and timeline performance
- Business development leads generated
- Brand/reputation impact (positive mentions, referrals)

---

### Ongoing Partnership Operations

**Communication Protocols**:

- Weekly status updates on active engagements
- Monthly business development pipeline review
- Quarterly strategic planning sessions
- Annual contract renewal and terms renegotiation

**Conflict Resolution**:

- Define escalation path for disagreements
- Commit to resolution within defined timeframe (e.g., 10 business days)
- Mediation before arbitration or litigation
- Exit option as final resolution mechanism

**Performance Management**:

- Define quality standards and SLAs
- Regular feedback on delivery quality
- Continuous improvement initiatives
- Consequences for performance failures (remediation, reduction in scope, termination)

**Relationship Evolution**:

- Annual review of partnership value and strategic fit
- Renegotiation of terms as business scales
- Expansion into new markets or capabilities
- Graceful exit if strategic fit deteriorates

## Partnership Termination Protocol

### When to Exit Partnership

**Exit Triggers**:

- Consistent failure to meet performance standards
- Repeated violations of agreed terms (client ownership, IP protection)
- Financial instability or ethical issues emerge
- Strategic misalignment (goals no longer compatible)
- Better alternative partners identified
- Partnership no longer economically viable

**Exit Process**:

1. **Notification**: Provide written notice per contract terms (30-90 days)
2. **Work-in-Progress**: Complete or transition active client engagements
3. **Client Communication**: Inform clients of transition with professionalism
4. **Financial Settlement**: Complete all outstanding payments and invoices
5. **IP Return**: Ensure all proprietary materials returned or destroyed
6. **Post-Termination**: Enforce non-solicit and confidentiality obligations

### Graceful Exit Communication

**Script for Partner**:

> "After reviewing our partnership performance over [time period], I've decided to [terminate / not renew] our agreement. This isn't a reflection on [positive aspects], but [specific reason: strategic shift, economic misalignment, etc.].
>
> Per our contract, I'm providing [X days] notice. I'm committed to professionally completing [active engagements] and ensuring smooth client transitions. I'll work with you on a transition plan.
>
> I appreciate [specific positive contributions] and wish you continued success."

**Script for Clients** (if joint engagements):

> "I wanted to inform you that my partnership with [Partner] is concluding. This doesn't affect our engagement—I remain your strategic advisor and will ensure continuity. [If applicable: I'll be working with [new partner] for technical delivery / I'll be completing remaining work with my internal team].
>
> You'll see no disruption in service quality or delivery timelines. Please reach out if you have any questions."
