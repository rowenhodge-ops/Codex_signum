## The Core Negotiation Dynamic

*   **Their Position (CDN/Org):** As the relationship holder, they want to maximize their margin, keep the client engagement simple, and present a unified front. They might see your `$15k Initium` as a complex, hard-to-sell "add-on" or an unnecessary cost that eats into the total project value. They may want to bundle everything under one simple price.
*   **Your Position (Codex Signum):** You need to get paid for your high-margin diagnostic (`Initium`), protect the integrity of your process (especially the `Colophon`), and ensure you have the profit to fund your "Hidden Business" (`Praxis Forge`). Your fixed-price model is only viable *because* of the de-risking done in the `Initium`.

## Scenario Modeling: How to Charge the Client & Split the Fees

Let's model this using a complete engagement based on your plan: a **$15k Initium** followed by a **$150k Fabrica**, for a **Total Client Price of $165,000**.

### Scenario A: The Ideal Model (You Prime the Contract)

This model aligns perfectly with your business plan and protects your IP. You position yourself as the "Strategic Architect" who brings in the "Delivery Partner."

*   **How it Works:** The client contract is with **Codex Signum for $165k**. You then subcontract work to Org and pay a "relationship" or "referral" fee to Christian/his firm.

*   **Financial Breakdown:**
    | Item | Revenue / (Cost) | Notes |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | **Client Invoice (to Codex Signum)** | **$165,000** | You own the client relationship legally. |
    | COGS: Org Delivery Fee | ($75,000) | Per your plan, 50% of the `Fabrica` price. |
    | COGS: CDN's "Relationship" Fee | ($16,500) | **Assumption:** 10% of total contract value. This is his reward for the relationship. |
    | COGS: Your Tools/Data | ($1,000) | Your cost for the `Initium`. |
    | **Total COGS** | **($92,500)** | |
    | **Gross Profit (for Codex Signum)** | **$72,500** | |
    | **Gross Margin** | **44%** | Healthy margin that funds Praxis Forge. |

*   **Pros:** **Maximum Control.** You enforce your process, terms, and the non-negotiable `Colophon`. Your brand is front-and-center.
*   **Cons:** **Harder to Negotiate.** CDN/Org may resist being a "subcontractor." It requires them to trust you completely with *their* client relationship.

### Scenario B: The Pragmatic Model (They Prime, You Are a "Strategic Expert")

This is a common compromise. They own the paper, and you are brought in as a high-value, fixed-fee expert for a specific, non-negotiable part of the process.

*   **How it Works:** CDN/Org signs the **$165k contract** with the client. They then have a separate SOW with you to deliver the "Diagnostic & Architectural Blueprint" phase.

*   **Financial Breakdown:**
    | Party | Revenue / (Cost) | Notes |
    | :--- | :--- | :--- |
    | **CDN / Org receives from Client** | **$165,000** | They own the client relationship. |
    | Fee Paid to Codex Signum | ($XX,XXX) | **This is the key negotiation point.** |
    | Org's Internal Delivery Cost | (~$75,000) | Their cost to deliver the `Fabrica` portion. |
    | CDN/Org's Gross Profit | **$XX,XXX** | |

    **Your Portion (The Negotiation):**
    *   **Your "Weak" Position:** You just sell the `$15k Initium`. You get **$14,000 profit**, but lose all control and visibility over the larger project and the `Colophon`. **This is unacceptable.**
    *   **Your "Strong" Position:** You charge for the `Initium` **plus** a "Strategic Governance & Value Validation" fee for the `Fabrica` phase.
        *   Your Fee = $15k (`Initium`) + $25k (`Fabrica` Governance & `Colophon`) = **$40,000**
        *   Your Profit = $40,000 - $1,000 (tools) = **$39,000**

*   **Pros:** **Easier to Sell.** It fits their existing mental model. They maintain primary control of the client, reducing their perceived risk.
*   **Cons:** **Significant IP & Process Risk.** They could try to squeeze your fee. They might see the `Colophon` as optional "paperwork" and fight you on it. You risk being commoditized over time.

---

### Scenario C: The "What They Might Propose" Model (Blended & Squeezed)

This is a risk scenario where they try to absorb your function and pay you a simple day rate or a minimal fee.

*   **How it Works:** They tell the client the price is "$165k for the whole project." Internally, they try to commoditize your contribution.
*   **Potential Offer to You:** "We'll pay you a fee of $10,000 for the two-week diagnostic phase." They subsume the rest of your margin and role into their "Project Management" or "Strategy" overhead.
*   **Financial Breakdown:** You receive **$10k**. Your profit is **$9k**. They make an extra **$5k** profit (and likely more by cutting your governance role).
*   **Result:** This model is a **non-starter**. It destroys your business model, prevents you from funding Praxis Forge, and eliminates the `Colophon`, which is the entire point of the "Visible Business."

## Recommendation & Negotiation Playbook

Your goal is to secure **Scenario A**. Be prepared to fall back to the "strong" version of **Scenario B** for a pilot project to build trust. Reject **Scenario C** outright.

Here is how you frame the discussion with Christian:

**1. Anchor on the Shared Goal: "De-risking Fixed-Price Work"**
*   Start here. "Christian, we both agree that fixed-price, value-based projects are the future. The biggest risk is scope creep and unclear requirements blowing up our margins. My `Initium` process is our **shared insurance policy**. It's the mechanism that guarantees we can deliver a $150k project profitably and without surprises."

**2. Justify the `$15k Initium` as a Value-Creator, Not a Cost**
*   He may say, "$15k is too high." Reframe it immediately.
*   **For the Client:** "This isn't a cost; it's an investment. In two weeks, for just $15k, the client gets a complete architectural blueprint of their problem and a guaranteed, fixed-price proposal for the solution. It gives them massive clarity and budget certainty."
*   **For Us (the Partnership):** "This $15k process is what creates a perfectly qualified, pre-sold $150k project for Org. It saves them weeks of pre-sales engineering and discovery calls. It's a high-ROI lead qualifier for the main project."

**3. Propose Scenario A as the "Cleanest Model"**
*   "The cleanest way to structure this is for Codex Signum to lead the engagement. I'll handle the architectural and fiduciary components (`Initium` and `Colophon`), and we'll subcontract the expert implementation to Org. For your role in originating and managing the relationship, my model accounts for a 10% fee on the total contract value. This way, everyone is paid for their specific expertise."

**4. If He Pushes Back, Pivot to the "Strong" Scenario B**
*   If he says, "No, we have to prime this, it's our client," then pivot.
*   "Okay, I understand. If you need to prime the contract, then we need to scope Codex Signum's role clearly. We will deliver two fixed-price components:
    1.  **The `$15k Initium`:** The upfront diagnostic and SOW creation.
    2.  **The `$25k Fabrica Governance Package`:** This covers our ongoing strategic oversight during the build and, most importantly, includes the **non-negotiable ROI validation via the `Colophon` at the end.** This protects all of us and proves the value we delivered.
*   So, out of the $165k client price, your SOW with me would be for **$40,000**."

This isn't just a fee discussion; it's a negotiation about the fundamental architecture of your partnership. By framing it around de-risking and value, you position your contribution as essential, not expensive.
